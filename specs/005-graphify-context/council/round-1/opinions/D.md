The deck's §7 already runs an unusually rigorous self-audit — 7d's tally (4/9 SCs cleanly committed, 3/9 split, 2/9 uncovered) and 7e's branch-coverage table are exactly the kind of adversarial self-check most decks skip. I cross-checked the specific claims worth doubting against the source documents (plan.md's arm-1 Fixture line at L66, the "Detach order (D74-2)" section at L113, the Testing line at L26, R1 in the risk register at L161; spec.md's FR-004/FR-011/FR-012, SC-002/SC-004/SC-006 wording) and found no drift — the deck represents plan.md and spec.md accurately everywhere I checked. That honesty is worth crediting, but it also means my job is to press past what's already disclosed, not just restate it.

Two of §7e's self-flagged branch-coverage gaps deserve to be elevated from "noted" to "blocking a testability sign-off," because they sit on the feature's two highest-stakes MUST-behaviors, not incidental ones. FR-004 requires the labeled-assertion fallback to fire — and be labeled — for every relationship the extractor can't model, and SC-002 demands "zero unlabeled fallback claims" (verified verbatim in spec.md); but the only fixture arm 1 has (plan.md L66) feeds it a known topology and checks the successful-modeling branch — nothing in the committed set feeds it an edge kind outside the three modeled ones and asserts the label appears instead of silence. Symmetrically, arm 2's stale-survivor guard is the mechanism built specifically to stop the M3 defect (86 stale duplicate nodes from an ungated merge) from recurring, and SC-004's fixture (b) only demonstrates 0-survivors on an ordinary refresh — no fixture manufactures survivors > 0 to prove prune-or-rebuild actually detects and recovers. A guard tested only on green input hasn't been shown to catch red input, which is the entire reason it exists.

I checked the graph on-demand for both gaps (`graphify query "SC-002 fallback labeling fixture coverage test"` and `graphify query "stale survivor guard prune-or-rebuild test"`), expecting silence since tasks.md doesn't exist yet and neither augment.sh nor refresh.sh is implemented. Both queries returned real nodes but none from 005 or naming these fixtures — the hits were semantically-adjacent 004-testing-completion nodes (run.sh, assert_coverage_catches(), etc.). That's the expected, disclosed graph-silence (S22, doubly), not a refutation of anything — it confirms these are plan-stage fixture-design gaps the graph can't adjudicate either way, so the finding rests on the deck/spec text itself, which grounds well (FR-004/SC-002 are concept-layer requirements I read directly).

Two more findings are mine, not the deck's. Plan.md L26 and the deck (§3, §7a) tie arm 1's "byte-deterministic" fixture claim to the absence of an LLM in the augmentation pass ("deterministic parsing (no LLM)"). That's necessary but not sufficient — byte-for-byte diffing also needs deterministic *serialization*: stable node/edge ordering, canonical JSON key order, no filesystem-iteration-order dependence. Nothing in plan.md or the deck states this discipline, and it's a classic flaky-golden-test source. Separately, SC-004's "materially lower cost" and SC-006's "spend... lower than an equivalent unbounded loop" are both structurally immune to failing a committed test — I grepped spec.md directly for a numeric threshold on either and found none, only qualitative "materially"/"lower." The deck's own 7c/7d tally marks both manual/measurement-only but doesn't connect them as the same underlying problem: an SC with no stated number can never go red, only get eyeballed. SC-006 at least has a real comparator already banked (this round's own uncapped baseline); SC-004 has no anchor at all yet.

Last: D74-2's severability claim — four independently-shippable arms with an explicit detach order — is itself a testability-relevant claim (do arms 2+3+4 actually ship and degrade gracefully with arm 1 detached, exactly as the fallback story in §2 asserts?), but none of the four per-arm fixtures (7a) or six consumer fixtures (7b) runs in a detached configuration; every fixture implicitly assumes all shipped arms are present together. Since "any detach is a triage disposition" is a real, named possibility rather than hypothetical, the severability story is presently rationale-only, not fixture-verified — the same prose-vs-mechanism gap the deck itself calls out elsewhere (§2's rejection of the "one sectioned file" alternative, D53).
