## Chairman's note

Two blocking defects gate this plan: `assemble.py`'s grant union is unsorted before serialization (S01), so it can emit byte-different rosters and defeat the SC-005 determinism the feature exists to guarantee; and nothing writes the `workforce` binding into `gates.yml` (S02) — verified by three sources reading the installed hook scripts rather than the deck's paraphrase — leaving `before_implement` and `/speckit-implement-parallel` permanently unreachable as scoped. The strong tier's real find, surfaced by the peer across members who could not see each other, is that S02, the missing approve command (S13), and the two-extension merge (S10) are one design decision: a single `workforce` extension is the natural home for a dedicated approval event and its gate-write hook, and the three should be triaged together. Members split only on severity, not merit — every graph-grounded claim (the "degree 15" correction S06, the D48 dead-branch S03) was independently reproduced, and no opinion fell below the bar.
