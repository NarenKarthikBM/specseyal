The deck's headline sequencing claim — categorize (inference) → `assemble.py` (code, deterministic) → skill-builder (inference, gap-only) → re-run → human sign → implement — reads cleanly, and the one ordering fact my brief asked me to pin down explicitly does hold up: `contracts/commands.md` states plainly "`categorize` (runs **after** `analyze` — D58)", matching the deck's Problem Restatement. Categorize-before-assign is also structurally true (assign's context-in is `categorization.md`).

Where the deck undersells itself is R7/R8 — "reusing git's `before_implement` workforce `verify-gate` hook" is framed as simple, already-solved reuse, and `commands.md` line 66 says the pair is "registering nothing new for gate freshness." I didn't take that at face value; I read the actual shipped mechanism it leans on. `speckit.git.record-gate`'s only existing hook-internal action, `on-council-approve.sh`, is hardcoded to the council gate, and its own header comment says outright: "it is hardcoded to the council gate because this hook only ever fires for the council gate (see extension.yml's other hooks for the workforce-gate equivalent, **still M3 future work**)." I also read `verify-gate.sh` in full: it fails closed on a missing `gates.yml` or an absent binding, by explicit design (R1-S10), never fails open. Put together: nothing currently specified anywhere writes a `workforce` binding into `gates.yml`, and the pair's own two-row hooks table (categorize/agent-assign → `git.commit` only, nothing → `record-gate`) confirms it. As scoped, `before_implement` can never pass. This is one of the two things the deck asks the council to actively pressure-test (R1's determinism boundary is the other) — I'd put this one at least as high.

Second-order finding from the same digging: fixing this means adding a script + hook entry into **002 git-ext's own source tree**, not just the shared `.specify/extensions.yml` registry — the one place a "patch a foreign extension" move looks actually necessary, when every other rejected alternative in the deck (correctly) avoids exactly that move. Since source (`extensions/git/extension/`) and installed (`.specify/extensions/git/`) are separate copies (confirmed in sync right now, same commit), that fix needs its own reinstall step or it lands inert in source only.

Smaller, well-grounded items below. One is a straight grounding correction: I ran `graphify explain ".specify/extensions.yml"` against the committed D59 baseline (confirmed byte-identical to `graphify-out/graph.json` — same `built_at_commit`, same 497/888 counts) and got degree **1**, not the "degree 15" my brief's framing implies is a graph fact. The underlying shared-registry risk is real (I independently confirmed ~12 existing hook entries across git/council/graphify by reading the installed file), but "15" isn't something the graph actually computed.
