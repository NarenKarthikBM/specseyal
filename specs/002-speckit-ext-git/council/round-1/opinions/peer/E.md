**Ranking.** D and B contain the round's two highest-leverage findings. D's headline — every git-ext hook, including the `before_tasks`/`before_implement` hard-blocks, is registered `optional: true` — I verified directly against `.claude/skills/speckit-tasks/SKILL.md` L33-55: an `optional: true` hook only ever renders a `Prompt:`/`To execute:` block, while the "you MUST actually invoke... wait for it to finish" compulsion is gated strictly to `optional: false`. `contracts/commands.md`'s hook table header is literally "registered in `.specify/extensions.yml`, `optional: true`" — applied uniformly, including to `verify-gate.sh`'s hard-block row. This is the single most consequential finding in the round because it's a *precondition* for every other sequencing claim anyone (including me, below) makes: it doesn't matter which hook point is chosen, or in what order hooks compose, if nothing compels the hook to fire at all. B is the strongest on genuinely novel concurrency/ordering hazards nobody else found (the `create-new-feature.sh` race, the task-marked-before-commit ordering hole) — both hold up under direct code inspection, see below. A is the most exhaustive (five cross-referenced findings, each with file+line citations) and its Finding 1 is the most-cited fact-pattern across the whole round, but its central "reconcile the decision" framing is resolved by a document A apparently didn't check (`docs/90` D51, ratified the same day as this plan) — see my adjudication below. C is comparatively thinner on hard "this breaks" findings — several of its suggestions are calibration/cost-accounting arguments (fair, but lower-stakes than a mechanism gap) — with one clear exception: its hook-composition/priority catch is as sharp as anything B or D found, and deserves to not be undersold just because C's assigned lens is simplicity.

**Adjudicating A vs. B on `before_specify` vs. `after_specify`.** Both cite the same two facts (`graphify-context.md` L13's "needs... `before_specify`"; `speckit-specify/SKILL.md`'s live `before_specify` contract) but draw different conclusions — A treats the after_specify choice as possibly wrong and worth reopening; B treats it as settled, with the two documents simply stale. I read `docs/90-DECISIONS-AND-IDEAS.md` D51 directly: it **ratifies** FR-001 as "branch created co-incident with `specify`... before `spec.md` is committed" and records `artifact-layout.md` §2/§3 as amended in the same commit, dated 2026-07-09 — today, and same-day as this plan. B's read is the accurate one: the decision is closed, not open. I also checked whether the plan's rejection of `before_specify` is a strawman, per A's implicit doubt — it isn't: `create-new-feature.sh` (which persists `feature.json`, the spec-ID source `after_specify` reads) is invoked *during* `specify`'s own Pre-Execution/Outline flow, so a `before_specify` hook structurally cannot read a spec ID that doesn't exist yet and would have to duplicate the NNN/slug logic exactly as `plan.md` §C claims. A's "switch to `before_specify`" suggestion isn't viable; the fix is entirely in A's other suggestion (fix the two stale documents) plus the interruption-window gap A also raises (still valid, see Finding 1's second half — untouched by this adjudication).

That said, re-reading D51's own language against the actual mechanism surfaces something neither A nor B said precisely: D51 ratifies branch birth "as soon as the spec ID is assigned," but the spec ID is assigned early — `speckit-specify/SKILL.md` persists `feature.json` around L78-101, right after Pre-Execution Checks — while `after_specify` (branch birth) doesn't fire until L238, the very end of the Outline, after drafting, up to 3 checklist-validation iterations, and a possible multi-turn clarification Q&A. The mechanism satisfies the letter of D51/`artifact-layout.md` §2 ("before `spec.md` is committed") but arguably not the "as soon as... is assigned" framing in the decision row itself — there's a whole drafting session in between. Functionally this is the same uncommitted-window hazard A's Finding 1 already flags in detail; what's new here is that the ratified decision's own wording oversells how early the mechanism actually runs, which is worth a one-line disambiguation in the decision record so a future reader doesn't expect branch creation to happen at ID-assignment time.

**Sharpening B's race-condition finding (Point 2).** I read `create-new-feature.sh` directly: `get_highest_from_specs()` is an unlocked `for dir in "$specs_dir"/*` scan, and the `[ -d "$FEATURE_DIR" ] && [ "$ALLOW_EXISTING" != true ]` guard is a genuine check-then-act gap before `mkdir -p` (which doesn't fail on an existing directory). The race is real. But B's headline scenario — "silently checks out onto the first racer's branch, merging two unrelated specs" — requires two racers to land on the *same NNN and the same slug*. The higher-probability outcome of the exact same race (two different feature descriptions, same stale `HIGHEST` read) is same-NNN-different-slug: two directories (e.g. `specs/003-feature-a/` and `specs/003-feature-b/`) silently coexisting under one shared number, with `mkdir -p` succeeding for both and no branch collision at all. That's a duller bug but a more likely one, and it needs a different fix — atomic/locked NNN allocation, not B's "fail loudly if the branch already exists" (which only catches the same-slug sub-case, since two different-slug racers never even collide on the `mkdir` call). Worth noting too: this TOCTOU predates git-ext entirely — it's a latent stock-`spec-kit` bug — 002 doesn't introduce it, it adds a sharper consequence (a git-branch collision) on top of a pre-existing one.

**Endorsing B's Point 3 without reservation.** The ordering hole between `tasks.md`'s per-task `[X]` marking (step 3) and the proposed per-wave commit call (step 5, per D-R7) in the shipped `speckit-implement-parallel/SKILL.md` loop is, on my re-reading, the single best resumability/sequencing catch in the round — it's the one place a resuming session genuinely cannot distinguish "wave done, commit missing" from "wave partial, discard" from git+`tasks.md` state alone. It deserves a named risk-register row of its own rather than living inside R1's general "command forgets to call the primitive" framing, which is a different failure mode (omission, not ordering).

**Endorsing C's hook-composition finding, confirmed independently.** I cross-referenced `extensions/graphify/extension/extension.yml` (registers exactly `before_plan`, `before_tasks`, `before_implement` — zero `after_*` hooks) against git-ext's own table (every commit hook is `after_specify/clarify/plan/tasks/implement` — none is a `before_*` hook). `plan.md` §F's claim — "the git commit hook runs after graphify's context hook at a shared boundary" — describes a composition that cannot occur under this design: a git *commit* hook never shares a key with a graphify hook, since graphify registers none of the `after_*` keys. The only two keys where both extensions are genuinely present are `before_tasks` and `before_implement` — and there, git's hook is `verify-gate` (a hard-block check), never a commit. `priority: N` is also never resolved to an actual number anywhere in `plan.md`, `research.md`, `data-model.md`, or `contracts/commands.md` — it's a placeholder throughout. Combined with D's `optional: true` finding, this means the real open question — should a cheap stale-gate check run before graphify pays for a full context-generation pass at those two shared points — isn't just unassigned a priority, it's not even guaranteed to run in *either* order, since both hooks at that boundary are merely "announced."

**On the grounding-gap convergence (A, C, and partly B).** These are two distinct findings and the chairman shouldn't merge them into one suggestion: A and C both found the *graph tool* can't see `.sh`/`.yml` files at all (reconfirmed above via two more `graphify explain` misses), so any deck claim about install.sh patterns or hook-key blast radius is unverifiable by graph regardless of truth. B's related but separate point is that one specific document's *content* — `graphify-context.md`'s `before_specify` line — is factually stale relative to a ratified decision. A tool-coverage gap and a stale-content bug are different problems with different fixes.
