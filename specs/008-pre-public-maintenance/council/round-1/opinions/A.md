Given that strength, the gaps worth flagging are precision and self-consistency issues, not fabrications.

**Path error.** §4's Project Structure table lists `git/install.sh, extension.yml, test/run.sh` as if all three are siblings directly under `extensions/git/`. They aren't: `install.sh` and `test/run.sh` are at that level, but `extension.yml` actually lives one directory deeper, at `extensions/git/extension/extension.yml` (verified with `ls`/`find`). For a deck whose entire credibility rests on graph-verified precision of exactly these kinds of paths, this is a real (if small) inaccuracy an implementer following the deck literally would trip over.

**A self-inconsistency between §2's summary and §7's detail.** §2 says the four hardenings each land "with a both-branch committed fixture (with two exceptions surfaced in §7)." But §7's own table, cross-checked against `data-model.md`'s "Cross-entity invariants" section (which names only E2/E4/E5 as covered by "both-branch fixture discipline" — I read that section directly), shows: I-23 (E4) has clean both-branch coverage; I-26 (E5) has a fixture but a known gap (the `dequote()` other-callers invariant is asserted, not fixture-proven); and I-29 (E6) / I-31 (E7) have **no committed fixture at all** — not a partial gap, a total absence. That's 3 of 4 hardening items with a real testability shortfall, not "two exceptions" against an otherwise-solid baseline. The phrase undersells what §7 itself documents.

**Risk Register omission.** The Risk Register (§5) gives the I-26/`dequote()` gap its own dedicated row — appropriately, since it's a real regression risk. But it gives no equivalent row to the strictly worse I-29/I-31 gap (zero fixture, not a partial one), even though §7 flags it explicitly as excluded from `data-model.md`'s own invariants list. A risk that severe enough to earn a full paragraph in §7 should earn a Risk Register row too, especially since SC-008/SC-009 are the two Success Criteria the deck itself marks "Manual" with no committed regression suite.

**Fixture-to-contract mapping is unstated.** §7's SC-004 row cites "committed both-branch fixtures ... (conformant/ + 4 violation-*/)" as satisfying FR-009 ("each injected contract violation fails naming the cause"). `data-model.md`'s E3 table lists six directly-checked contracts (the three delegated ones inherit their existing validators' own fixtures). Nothing in the deck states whether the 4 violation dirs map one-to-one onto those six, bundle multiple violations per dir, or leave some direct-check contracts (e.g. `trace-schema.md`, `testing-doc.md`) without any injected-violation exercise. This isn't necessarily wrong, but it's a claim the deck asserts as satisfied without showing the mapping — worth resolving explicitly before `/speckit-tasks` locks the fixture set.
