All five stage-1 opinions hold up on inspection — no wasted seats, no unsupported alarm-ringing — but they are not uniformly strong, and reading them as a set surfaces two near-unanimous findings plus a couple of cross-opinion contradictions no single lens-holder could see alone.

**Ranking as a set.** E (sequencing) and D (testability) are the strongest. E's resolution of the apparent wave-1 bootstrap paradox — `verify-gate.sh`'s pre-wave-1 firing checks a pristine `tasks.md` and passes regardless of whether the fix is live, so the defect is only ever *triggered* by wave 1's own commit, leaving wave 1 itself as a safe build/prove window — is the single most technically substantive individual contribution in the round; neither deck §3 nor plan.md §2.3 states this reasoning, they only assert the wave-1 requirement. D is equally strong on citation discipline: the SC-008-vs-SC-010 regression-rigor asymmetry and the "zero hand assistance is a process claim, not an outcome claim" point are both sharp, well-cited, and actionable. A is solid throughout with one standout catch (the context-retention-vs-resumability tension) and a well-scoped, non-overclaiming fixture-gap finding. B is the strongest of the five specifically on Concern 1's risk surface — the tester's second-hand-honesty framing and the R2-mitigation-vs-trace-schema mismatch are the two sharpest *new* risk points anyone raises all round — but overreaches once (the "direction-agnostic" reading of the checkbox rule, challenged below). C is the most disciplined reviewer in the "don't manufacture complaints" sense and lands the round's cleanest convention-grounded ratification (`/speckit-complete`-as-command, independently verified against the live `/speckit-*` command roster), but its headline simplicity proposal — replacing the diff classifier with a canonicalized-checkbox hash — contains a real correctness gap that only surfaces when read against A's own flip-direction question (detailed below). No opinion is weak; the spread is narrow.

**The two headline findings.** (1) Every single one of the five — five different lenses, writing before seeing each other — independently flags that FR-016/SC-010's "wave 1 (or pre-wave)" placement has no mechanical enforcement anywhere in deck or plan.md, since `tasks.md` doesn't exist yet. That is an unusually strong evidentiary signal for an adversarial council; the chairman should treat it as close to settled rather than one risk among many. (2) Four of five (all but E, whose lens stayed concern-2-focused) independently converge on the same fix for R1/SC-004: mechanically grep `spec.md`'s `SC-\d+`/`FR-\d+` IDs against `testing.md`'s coverage map. Also cheap, also non-controversial.

**Weighing both concerns.** Concern 2 (I-17) draws findings that concentrate almost entirely on one mechanism — wave-1 placement — but with total convergence. Concern 1 (the testing extension) draws more numerous but more distributed findings (SC-004, SC-005's unconfirmed appendix pair, SC-008's under-specified regression, the tester's evidentiary fragility, silent-hook-failure handling) — each individually well-argued but none commanding full consensus.
