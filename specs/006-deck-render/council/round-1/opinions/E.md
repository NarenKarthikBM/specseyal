> **Reduced grounding** — no graphify-out/graph.json found; this review is deck-only.

The deck's central argument — "the seam cannot rot and cannot collide with `005`'s concurrent rewrite ... by construction, not by luck" (§2, §4 Structure Decision) — holds up on file-level grounds: zero hooks, a disjoint extension tree, and a five-file blast list outside it. But sequencing risk in this feature doesn't live at the file level; it lives in two places the deck under-treats: the loop semantics of `deck_render: both`, and the coupling `spec.md`'s own Sequencing note admits runs in the *opposite* direction from what §5's risk register frames.

On the `both`-mode loop: §3 step 4 says "for each selected deck, in order" and separately claims exit code 2 fires when one deck renders and one fails (§3 "Exit codes"). Nothing in the stated I1–I7 invariants or the step list actually guarantees that a failure on deck 1 doesn't abort the loop before deck 2 is even attempted — which would silently collapse the exit-2 path into exit-4 territory and violate O2 ("silence is never an acceptable degradation") by omission rather than by a coded bug. §7c catches the adjacent problem (no fixture exercises this branch) and explicitly calls it out as worth confirming — I'd escalate that self-flagged ambiguity, not just endorse it, because the missing fixture and the missing loop-isolation invariant are the same gap seen from two angles: one is "we don't test it," the other is "we never actually promised it."

On the reverse coupling: `spec.md`'s Sequencing note (line 181) states plainly that `005` "books the after-measurement of its arm-4 query ceiling to the *next* council round, which is `006`'s" — i.e., **this round** is meant to serve as `005`'s measurement instrument, not the other way around. The deck's own §5 risk row 4 restates this but frames the risk narrowly as a merge-order scheduling question ("Out of this spec's hands"). It misses the sharper problem: I independently confirmed `graphify-out/graph.json` is absent, exactly as the deck's own §4 discloses. If `005`'s arm-4 measurement requires graph queries happening during a council round, that measurement cannot occur in *this* round regardless of whether `005` merges before or after — the instrument spec.md is counting on is graph-less today. That's a sequencing hazard the risk register doesn't actually name.

Separately, on the config-parsing boundary (still a sequencing-adjacent ordering-of-checks issue): §3 step 2 collapses "file/key absent" and "file/key **unreadable**" into the same silent `none` branch. `spec.md` FR-006 (line 109) only authorizes two branches — absent ⇒ none, out-of-enum ⇒ must fail validation — and says nothing about a parse failure. A profile.yaml that fails to parse is a worse signal than one that parses to a bad string, yet the deck routes it into the quieter branch instead of the loud one SC-008 promises for bad values.

- [testability] The `deck_render: both` partial-failure branch (one deck rendered, one failed ⇒ exit 2) has no committed fixture anywhere in the Project Structure tree — only a symmetric two-good-decks fixture plus the enum-guard fixtures exist. §7c already flags this as "AMBIGUOUS ... worth flagging to the council"; treat that self-flag as a blocking gap, not a footnote. (confidence: high)
- [sequencing] The per-deck loop's isolation guarantee — that an exception processing deck N does not prevent deck N+1 from being attempted and disclosed — is implied by "for each selected deck, in order" (§3 step 4) but is not stated among the I1–I7 invariants. This is the exact property exit code 2 and O2 depend on; it should be named explicitly, not left implicit. (confidence: high)
- [sequencing] `spec.md`'s Sequencing note (line 181) frames `006`'s council round as `005`'s arm-4 measurement instrument, but `graphify-out/graph.json` is confirmed absent for this very round (independently verified; also disclosed in the deck's own §4). The measurement `005` is counting on cannot happen in this round regardless of merge order — the risk register (§5, row 4) rates this "Out of this spec's hands" without noting the instrument itself is currently graph-less. (confidence: high)
- [correctness] §3 step 2's "else `none` if the file/key is absent **or unreadable**" adds a third branch beyond what FR-006 (spec.md line 109) authorizes — FR-006 only distinguishes absent (⇒ none) from out-of-enum (⇒ must fail validation), with no "unreadable"/parse-failure case. A malformed profile.yaml is arguably a stronger failure signal than a valid-but-wrong string, yet it's routed to the silent branch instead of SC-008's loud one. Worth the council settling explicitly rather than leaving to implementer discretion. (confidence: high)
- [risk] No mechanism stamps which commit of `extensions/council/` actually produced this round's mechanics. If `005` merges its rewrite of council internals while this round is between deck-prep and chairman synthesis, there is no way to later detect that the reviewing apparatus itself shifted mid-round. A cheap fix (record the council-extension commit alongside round artifacts) is not in the current design. (confidence: med)
