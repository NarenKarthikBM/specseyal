## Chairman's note

All five members endorse the plan's central bet — a self-contained, zero-hook, model-free extension with gitignored output, whose boundary properties fall out of the shape rather than needing discipline — and none treats the rejected-alternatives table as a strawman; the round found no reason to reopen the architecture, and the whole council ran deck-only with no graph, which the banner above discloses. The blocking core is narrow and coherent, and every item is checkable against committed text without a graph: the config-resolution ladder silently swallows a malformed `profile.yaml` in the exact way FR-006/SC-008 forbid for a bad value (S01, 4/5 members); the `deck_render: both` partial-failure path is neither promised in the invariants nor forced by a fixture nor fully mapped in the exit-code contract (S02 unanimous, S03, S05); and O5's "never a partial file" guarantee is contradicted by its own non-atomic write mechanism, with the only forced-failure test tripping before the write begins (S04). Members converged with one contest — peer rebutted the alleged §4/§5 contradiction over the shared `extensions.yml` merge point and preferred the actionable framing (S11 over S15) — and the strongest single find, unanimous across all five lenses, is the one the deck honestly flagged for the council itself.
